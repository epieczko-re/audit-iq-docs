[[sect-regulatory-compliance]]
== Regulatory Compliance Hierarchy and Policy Management

Regulations do not exist in isolation.
A regulator creates a rule, then publishes guidance interpreting that rule.
The institution subject to the regulation writes internal policies and procedures to comply with the rule and its guidance.
An audit must verify compliance not merely against the raw regulation, but against the institution's own policies and procedures — because that is what examiners actually test against.

This section specifies how AuditIQ models this full hierarchy and how institution-specific policies and procedures are incorporated into the audit rule set.
For the base Audit Pack architecture, rule engine, and HMDA field coverage, see <<sect-audit-pack-spec>>.

[[sect-regulatory-compliance-hierarchy]]
=== The Compliance Hierarchy

.Regulatory Compliance Hierarchy — From Rule to Audit
[mermaid]
----

*Layer 1 — Regulation:* A federal or state rule with the force of law.
This is the regulatory floor — every institution subject to the regulation must comply.
Example: 12 CFR 1003 (Regulation C) governs HMDA data collection and reporting.

*Layer 2 — Regulatory Guidance:* Interpretive guidance published by the regulator to clarify how the rule should be implemented and examined.
Guidance does not have the force of law, but examiners use it as their playbook.
Example: CFPB HMDA Examination Procedures, FFIEC Interagency Guidelines.

*Layer 3 — Institution Policy:* The institution's board-approved compliance policy that addresses how it will comply with the regulation and guidance.
Policies are typically high-level statements of intent, risk appetite, and organizational responsibilities.
Example: "ABC Bank HMDA Data Integrity Policy" stating that all HMDA data will be validated against source documents within 30 days of closing.

*Layer 4 — Institution Procedures:* Operational procedures that implement the policy — the step-by-step instructions for staff.
Procedures are where the institution's specific thresholds, tolerances, escalation paths, and documentation requirements live.
Example: "ABC Bank HMDA Data Collection Procedures" specifying that loan amount discrepancies exceeding $25 require supervisor review (stricter than the regulatory standard).

*Layer 5 — Audit Rule Set:* The effective rules used to perform the audit.
This is the composition of the base Regulatory Pack (Layers 1–2) and the Institution Policy Profile (Layers 3–4).
The pack provides the regulatory floor; the policy profile tightens, extends, and contextualizes it for the specific institution.

=== Why Institution Policies Matter for Auditing

Examiners do not audit solely against the regulation.
They audit against the institution's own policies and procedures — and then assess whether those policies adequately implement the regulation.
This creates two distinct audit dimensions:

. *Regulatory compliance:* Does the institution's data and documentation meet the regulatory requirements?
(Answered by the base Regulatory Pack.)
. *Policy adherence:* Does the institution follow its own policies and procedures?
(Answered by the Institution Policy Profile overlay.)

An institution can be in regulatory compliance but in violation of its own policy.
An examiner will cite both.
AuditIQ must test both.

=== Three-Artifact Design

AuditIQ models this hierarchy with three distinct artifacts that compose at runtime:

.Three-Artifact Composition Model
[mermaid]
----
graph LR
  A["<b>Regulatory Pack</b><br/>Authored by RiskExec<br/>Regulatory floor (Layers 1–2)<br/>Immutable, versioned"] -->|Merge| C["<b>Effective Rule Set</b><br/>Computed at runtime<br/>What the audit runs against<br/>Deterministic composition"]
  B["<b>Institution Policy Profile</b><br/>AI-generated, human-approved<br/>Institution-specific (Layers 3–4)<br/>Immutable, versioned"] -->|Overlay| C

  style A fill:#4A6FA5,color:#fff,stroke:#2B4570
  style B fill:#8B5E3C,color:#fff,stroke:#5C3A1E
  style C fill:#2E8B57,color:#fff,stroke:#1B5E3B
----

*Artifact 1 — Regulatory Pack (existing):* The base pack as specified in <<sect-audit-pack-spec>>.
Contains all rules derived from the regulation and regulatory guidance.
Authored exclusively by RiskExec.
This is the regulatory floor — it cannot be weakened by any overlay.

*Artifact 2 — Institution Policy Profile (new):* A structured, versioned configuration artifact that captures the institution's specific policies and procedures for the regulation the pack covers.
Created via the AI-driven pipeline (see <<sect-ai-policy-pipeline>>) and approved by human compliance experts.
The profile can tighten, extend, and contextualize the base pack, but it cannot weaken the regulatory floor.

*Artifact 3 — Effective Rule Set (computed):* The deterministic merge of the Regulatory Pack and the Institution Policy Profile.
This is what the audit engine actually executes.
The merge is computed at audit-instance creation time and is immutable for the duration of the audit run.
See <<sect-effective-rule-merge>> for the merge logic specification.

=== Profile Lifecycle

Institution Policy Profiles follow the same lifecycle states as packs: Draft → Published → Deprecated → Retired.

.Institution Policy Profile Lifecycle
[mermaid]
----
stateDiagram-v2
    [*] --> Draft : AI generates draft from policy documents
    Draft --> Draft : Human review / AI re-analysis
    Draft --> Published : Compliance expert approval (RiskExec + Institution)
    Published --> Deprecated : Policy updated OR base pack major version change
    Deprecated --> Draft : New version initiated
    Deprecated --> Retired : Grace period expired
    Retired --> [*]
----

*Version pinning:* Each audit run is pinned to a specific (pack_version, profile_version) pair.
If either artifact is updated, new audit runs use the new versions; existing runs are unaffected.

*Compatibility:* The `base_pack_version_constraint` field in the profile ensures that a profile is only applied to compatible pack versions.
When a new MAJOR pack version is published, existing profiles must be re-validated and potentially re-generated.

==== Phase 1 — Policy Document Intake and Classification

The institution uploads their compliance policy and procedure documents.
These may include:

* Board-approved compliance policies (e.g., HMDA Data Integrity Policy)
* Operating procedures (e.g., HMDA Data Collection Procedures)
* Job aids and desk references
* Board resolutions related to compliance
* Risk appetite statements
* Internal audit findings and corrective action plans

AI classifies each uploaded document using the existing document processing pipeline (Docling-Graph — see <<sect-docling-graph>>).
Classification assigns:

* *Document type:* board_policy, compliance_policy, operating_procedure, job_aid, board_resolution, risk_appetite_statement
* *Regulatory domain(s):* which regulations the document addresses (matched against the base pack's `regulation` field)
* *Effective date and version:* extracted from document metadata or cover page
* *Extraction confidence:* overall confidence score for the AI's ability to parse and extract from this document

==== Phase 2 — AI-Driven Policy Requirement Extraction

AI reads each classified policy document and extracts *policy-level requirements* — not loan-level field data.
This is a distinct extraction task from the standard document processing pipeline.

*What AI extracts from policy documents:*

[cols="30%,35%,35%",options="header"]
|===
|Requirement Type |Description |Example

|Thresholds and tolerances
|Numeric limits that are stricter than the regulatory minimum
|"Loan amount discrepancies exceeding $25 require supervisor escalation"

|Procedural requirements
|Steps or approvals mandated by the institution's procedures
|"Rate-lock exceptions require dual sign-off from originator and branch manager within 24 hours"

|Documentation requirements
|Documents or records that must be present in the loan file
|"Signed disclosure acknowledgment must be filed within 3 business days of application"

|Exception criteria
|Conditions under which standard procedures are overridden
|"Manual overrides of automated underwriting require VP-level approval and written justification"

|Internal escalation triggers
|Conditions that trigger internal review or escalation
|"Any HMDA data error rate exceeding 2% triggers a full population re-review"

|Timing and deadline requirements
|Institution-specific deadlines beyond regulatory requirements
|"QC review must be completed within 15 days of closing"

|Risk appetite parameters
|Board-level risk tolerance statements affecting audit scope
|"Zero tolerance for HMDA reporting errors on government-monitored loan types"
|===

Each extracted requirement carries:

* A source citation (document_id, section, page, verbatim quote)
* An extraction confidence score
* A preliminary classification (threshold, procedural, documentation, exception, escalation, timing)

*Alignment categories:*

* *Tightening:* The policy imposes a stricter threshold or higher severity than the regulation requires.
A `threshold_override` entry is generated.
* *Extension:* The policy adds requirements that go beyond the scope of the base pack rule (e.g., additional approval steps, documentation checks).
A `supplemental_rule` or `procedural_checkpoint` entry is generated.
* *Gap:* The base pack contains a regulatory rule, but no corresponding policy language was found in the institution's documents.
A `gap_analysis` entry is generated.
This is a valuable compliance finding — it may indicate the institution needs to strengthen its policy framework.

*Alignment confidence:* Each alignment carries a confidence score based on the semantic similarity between the pack rule and the retrieved policy language.
Low-confidence alignments are flagged for mandatory human review in Phase 5.

Additionally, AI performs an *unanchored scan* — searching the policy documents for requirements that do not map to any existing base pack rule.
These become candidate supplemental rules with no regulatory anchor, classified as `beyond_regulatory_scope` in the traceability mapping.

==== Phase 4 — Draft Profile Generation

AI assembles the structured draft Institution Policy Profile from the Phase 2 and Phase 3 outputs:

* `policy_document_registry` — populated from Phase 1 classification
* `threshold_overrides` — generated from Phase 3 tightenings
* `supplemental_rules` — generated from Phase 2 extensions and Phase 3 unanchored findings
* `procedural_checkpoints` — generated from Phase 2 procedural/documentation/timing requirements
* `policy_to_regulation_traceability` — generated from Phase 3 alignments
* `gap_analysis` — generated from Phase 3 gaps

Every entry is tagged with `ai_suggested: true` and `approved: false`.
No AI-generated entry becomes effective until a human sets `approved: true`.

*Review actions per entry:*

* *Accept:* Entry is correct as generated.
Reviewer sets `approved: true`.
* *Modify:* Entry is directionally correct but needs adjustment (e.g., AI extracted the wrong threshold value).

* *Reject:* Entry is incorrect or irrelevant.
Entry is removed from the profile.
* *Escalate:* Entry requires further analysis — the reviewer is unsure whether the policy language supports the AI's interpretation.
Entry is flagged for deeper review.

*Mandatory review triggers:*

* Any entry with extraction confidence < 0.70
* Any threshold override that changes severity from informational/low to high/critical
* Any supplemental rule with `comparison_type: expression` (CEL-based)
* All gap analysis findings (gaps require institution acknowledgment)

*Dual approval requirement:* The profile requires approval from both a RiskExec compliance expert (validates regulatory accuracy) and an institution compliance officer (validates policy interpretation).
Neither party alone can publish the profile.

==== Phase 6 — Ongoing Policy Change Detection

When an institution updates its policies or procedures, the profile must be re-evaluated.

* The institution uploads the updated policy documents.
* AI performs a diff analysis against the prior version of the policy documents.
* AI identifies which profile entries are affected by the policy changes.
* A draft profile update is generated containing only the deltas — new overrides, modified thresholds, removed entries.
* The update goes through the same human review and dual approval process.
* The profile version is incremented (MINOR for additions/tightenings, MAJOR for structural changes).

*Trigger: Base pack update.*
When RiskExec publishes a new version of the base Regulatory Pack, all Institution Policy Profiles linked to that pack are automatically flagged for re-validation.
The AI pipeline re-runs Phase 3 (alignment) against the new pack rules and generates a delta report.

=== Effective Rule Set — Merge Logic

The Effective Rule Set is computed deterministically at audit-instance creation time by merging the base Regulatory Pack with the Institution Policy Profile.

.Effective Rule Set Merge Algorithm
[mermaid]
----
graph TD
  A["Load base Regulatory Pack<br/>(pack_id, pack_version)"] --> B["Load Institution Policy Profile<br/>(profile_id, profile_version)"]
  B --> C["Validate profile compatibility<br/>(base_pack_version_constraint)"]
  C --> D["Apply threshold overrides<br/><i>Only tightenings allowed</i>"]
  D --> E["Inject supplemental rules<br/><i>Prefixed with 'policy-'</i>"]
  E --> F["Inject procedural checkpoints<br/><i>Verification methods assigned</i>"]
  F --> G["Tag all rules with origin<br/><i>regulatory | policy_tightened | policy_supplemental</i>"]
  G --> H["Freeze Effective Rule Set<br/><i>Immutable for audit run duration</i>"]

  style A fill:#4A6FA5,color:#fff,stroke:#2B4570
  style B fill:#8B5E3C,color:#fff,stroke:#5C3A1E
  style C fill:#4A6FA5,color:#fff,stroke:#2B4570
  style D fill:#2E8B57,color:#fff,stroke:#1B5E3B
  style E fill:#2E8B57,color:#fff,stroke:#1B5E3B
  style F fill:#2E8B57,color:#fff,stroke:#1B5E3B
  style G fill:#2E8B57,color:#fff,stroke:#1B5E3B
  style H fill:#2E8B57,color:#fff,stroke:#1B5E3B
----

*Merge rules:*

. *Threshold overrides are applied in one direction only.*
A policy profile can reduce a tolerance (e.g., $50 → $25), escalate a severity (e.g., medium → high), or raise a confidence threshold.
It cannot widen a tolerance, downgrade a severity, or lower a confidence threshold.
If a profile attempts to weaken a regulatory rule, the merge rejects the override and logs a validation error.
. *Supplemental rules are injected alongside base pack rules.*
Supplemental rule IDs are prefixed with `policy-` to distinguish them from regulatory rules in findings and reports.
Supplemental rules are executed by the same rule engine with the same confidence propagation model.
. *Procedural checkpoints are injected as a distinct rule category.*
Checkpoints may use `ai_document_detection` (the AI pipeline checks for the presence of a required document), `field_check` (deterministic field comparison), or `manual_attestation` (requires human attestation during the audit).
. *Every rule in the Effective Rule Set is tagged with its origin:*
* `regulatory` — rule from the base pack, unmodified
* `policy_tightened` — base pack rule with a policy-driven threshold override
* `policy_supplemental` — supplemental rule from the policy profile
* `procedural_checkpoint` — procedural checkpoint from the policy profile
. *The Effective Rule Set is immutable for the duration of the audit run.*
If either the pack or the profile is updated mid-audit, the running audit continues with the original Effective Rule Set.
New audit runs pick up the latest versions.

*Finding attribution:* Every finding produced by the audit engine includes an `origin` field indicating whether it was triggered by a regulatory rule, a policy-tightened rule, a policy-supplemental rule, or a procedural checkpoint.
This allows examiners to clearly distinguish regulatory violations from policy adherence issues in the exam-ready bundle.
