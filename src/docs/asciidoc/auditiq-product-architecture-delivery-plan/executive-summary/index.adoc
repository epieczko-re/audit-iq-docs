== Executive Summary

AuditIQ is a new module for the RiskExec platform that transforms how financial institutions perform lending quality control and compliance audits.
It replaces manual “stare-and-compare” audit workflows — where reviewers tediously compare loan data fields against source documents using spreadsheets and email — with a structured, confidence-scored, evidence-managed platform that serves all three lines of defense (QC, compliance, and audit) in a single system.

*Why now:* Over 20% of existing RiskExec clients are already performing audit functions inside the platform using user-defined fields — a clear signal of unmet demand.
The team is validating AuditIQ with 8–10 design partners and publishing credible launch timelines.

*What makes it different from the primary competitor:*

* Three-layer confidence scoring (mapping, extraction, rule application) gives reviewers quantified trust signals, not binary pass/fail.
* Audit pack architecture makes the platform extensible by domain — each regulation gets a modular “pack” rather than hard-coded logic.
* Governance-first AI: every AI output is advisory, explainable, human-approved, and immutably logged.
* Three-lines-of-defense support in one system, vs. the primary competitor’s QC-only focus.

*Phase 1 MVP scope:* HMDA origination audit.
Manual and OCR-assisted review modes.
Findings management with remediation tracking.
Manager dashboards.
AI-assisted reporting.
Exam-ready export bundle.
Target: June 30, 2026.

*Revenue opportunity:* $3–4M incremental ARR within 24 months at base-case 40 customers and $90K average ACV.
Pricing model (<<sect-pricing>>) mirrors the primary competitor’s proven per-pack subscription tiers at 15–20% below incumbent rates, with AI governance controls included.
The primary competitor’s ~141 customers average $135K ACV (banks: $167K; top logos: $481K–$934K); AuditIQ targets the same ACV range with superior value.
