== Executive Summary

Financial institutions are bleeding time, money, and credibility on a compliance process that hasn't fundamentally changed in twenty years.
Every day, thousands of compliance officers sit in front of dual monitors, toggling between PDFs and spreadsheets, manually comparing loan data fields against source documents — one cell at a time, one document at a time, one prayer at a time that they don't miss the discrepancy that triggers an enforcement action.

*This is unacceptable.* And it is exactly the problem AuditIQ was built to kill.

AuditIQ is a new module for the RiskExec platform that replaces the "stare-and-compare" audit workflow — the drudgery of spreadsheets, email chains, and shared-drive archaeology — with a structured, confidence-scored, evidence-managed platform that serves all three lines per the IIA Three Lines Model (QC, compliance, and internal audit) in a single system.
It does not merely digitize the existing process; it fundamentally re-architects how compliance teams produce, manage, and defend their audit work.

*Why now — and why this cannot wait:*

* Over 20% of existing RiskExec clients are already performing audit functions inside the platform using user-defined fields — hacking workarounds because nothing else on the market serves their needs.
That is not a feature request; that is a fire alarm.
* The team is validating AuditIQ with 8–10 design partners who have told us, in no uncertain terms, that they are ready to move.
* The incumbent (ACES) is entrenched but vulnerable — bolted-on AI, QC-only scope, and a legacy architecture that cannot deliver what regulators now demand.
The window to take market share is open _right now_, and it will not stay open forever.

*What makes AuditIQ a category-defining product:*

* *Three-layer confidence scoring* (mapping, extraction, rule application) gives reviewers quantified trust signals — not binary pass/fail, but genuine transparency into _why_ the system reached its conclusion and _how much_ to trust it.
* *Audit pack architecture* makes the platform extensible by regulation — each regulatory domain gets a modular "pack" rather than hard-coded logic, enabling rapid expansion without re-engineering.
* *Governance-first AI:* every AI output is advisory, explainable, human-approved, and immutably logged.
This is not AI for AI's sake — this is AI that a regulator can examine and a compliance officer can defend.
* *IIA Three Lines Model support* in one system, vs. the primary competitor's QC-only tunnel vision.
First-line (QC), second-line (compliance), and third-line (internal audit) roles each maintain independent judgment while working from a single evidence repository, a unified audit trail, and a common language of confidence — for the first time.
