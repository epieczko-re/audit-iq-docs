== 1. Executive Summary

AuditIQ is a new module for the RiskExec platform that transforms how financial institutions perform lending quality control and compliance audits. It replaces manual ``stare-and-compare'' audit workflows — where reviewers tediously compare loan data fields against source documents using spreadsheets and email — with a structured, confidence-scored, evidence-managed platform that serves all three lines of defense (QC, compliance, and audit) in a single system.

*Why now:* Over 20% of existing RiskExec clients are already performing audit functions inside the platform using user-defined fields — a clear signal of unmet demand. The Board has directed the team to validate AuditIQ with 8–10 design partners and publish credible launch timelines. (Source: 2026_02.10 - CEO Letter)

*What makes it different from ACES (primary competitor):*

* Three-layer confidence scoring (mapping, extraction, rule application) gives reviewers quantified trust signals, not binary pass/fail.
* Audit pack architecture makes the platform extensible by domain — each regulation gets a modular ``pack'' rather than hard-coded logic.
* Governance-first AI: every AI output is advisory, explainable, human-approved, and immutably logged.
* Three-lines-of-defense support in one system, vs. ACES’s QC-only focus.

*Phase 1 MVP scope:* HMDA origination audit. Manual and OCR-assisted review modes. Findings management with remediation tracking. Manager dashboards. AI-assisted reporting. Exam-ready export bundle. Target: June 30, 2026.

*Revenue opportunity:* $3–4M incremental ARR within 24 months at base-case 40 customers and $90K average ACV. Pricing model (Section 13) mirrors ACES’s proven per-pack subscription tiers at 15–20% below ACES rates, with AI governance controls included. ACES’s 141 customers average $135K ACV (banks: $167K; top logos: $481K–$934K); AuditIQ targets the same ACV range with superior value. (Sources: 5 Audit-IQ - RiskExec New Product Brief.pdf, Internal ACES Pricing Study)
